Introduction

Let’s face it: laundry seems simple until it goes wrong. Shrunk shirts, faded colors, or that lingering damp smell – we’ve all been there. At Truewash Laundromat in Akoka, Lagos, we’ve seen every mistake in the book – and we’ve mastered how to fix them. If you’re tired of ruining your favorite clothes or just want your laundry to last longer, this detailed guide is for you:

1. Overloading the Machine

Overloading is one of the most common laundry mistakes. People often assume cramming more clothes into the washing machine will save time, but it’s the opposite. Clothes don’t get cleaned properly because they can’t move freely, and detergent can’t circulate evenly. This not only leaves your clothes dirty but also puts stress on your machine, reducing its lifespan.

At Truewash, we use professional-grade washing machines with specific load capacity limits. Our trained staff never overloads a machine. Instead, we separate heavy and light items, ensuring every load gets cleaned thoroughly and gently.

2. Using Too Much Detergent

You might think more soap means cleaner clothes, but excess detergent leaves residue on fabrics, makes clothes stiff, and can irritate the skin. Over time, it can also damage your washing machine.

At Truewash, we use high-efficiency, eco-friendly detergents in just the right amount. We measure based on load size and soil level to keep your garments fresh, soft, and skin-safe. But excess detergent leaves residue on fabrics, makes clothes stiff, and can irritate the skin. According to Consumer Reports, overusing detergent can also reduce washer performance and waste energy.

3. Washing All Fabrics Together

Every fabric has different needs. Washing towels with silk or jeans with delicate blouses can cause friction damage, pilling, or even color transfer.

At Truewash, we separate laundry based on fabric type, weight, and color. Each category is washed using specific settings and wash cycles to maintain fabric integrity and color vibrancy.

4. Ignoring Care Labels

Those little tags on your clothes exist for a reason. They provide crucial instructions on how to wash, dry, and iron a garment. Ignoring them can lead to shrinkage, fading, or permanent fabric damage.

At Truewash, we inspect care labels on every garment. This guides us on temperature settings, detergents to use, and whether to dry or air-dry. Our attention to detail ensures your items last longer and look newer. On how to wash, dry, and iron a garment. For a full breakdown of fabric symbols, see Tide’s fabric care guide.

5. Leaving Wet Clothes Too Long

We’ve all forgotten a load in the washer. But wet clothes that sit too long develop mildew, foul odors, and may even get re-stained by dirty water.

At Truewash, we have a time-sensitive laundry workflow. Each washed load goes into the dryer immediately, minimizing moisture buildup. For air-dry items, we hang them promptly in a ventilated space.

6. Using Hot Water on Everything

Hot water might feel like the safest choice, but it can shrink certain fabrics, fade colors, and damage elastic materials.

At Truewash, we assess each load before selecting a water temperature. Whites or heavily soiled clothes may need warmer water, while colors and delicate fabrics are cleaned with cold or lukewarm water to preserve texture and dye.

8. Not Separating Whites and Colors

Mixing whites with dark or bright colors often results in dye transfer. This leads to discolored whites and overall dullness.

At Truewash, color separation is standard practice. Whites are washed separately with the appropriate detergents, and color-brightening boosters are added when needed. This keeps whites crisp and colors vivid.https://tide.com/en-us/how-to-wash-clothes/fabrics

9. Improper Drying

Drying everything on high heat is a recipe for disaster. It can cause shrinking, stretching, or damage to fabrics like spandex, silk, and rayon.

At Truewash, we use commercial dryers with customizable settings. Delicate items are air-dried or tumbled on low heat. Our goal is to preserve the fit, shape, and feel of every garment.

10. Delaying Laundry Too Long

Letting laundry pile up for weeks creates deeply embedded odors, hard-to-remove stains, and even fabric deterioration. Plus, large loads are harder to sort and clean effectively.

Our service helps you stay consistent with laundry, even when life gets busy. We pick up, clean, and deliver on schedule – so you never have to worry about laundry again.
